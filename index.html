Research

Assessing Correlation Between Night-Time Light and Road Infrastructure

Collaborators: Sharmin Ahmed, Summit Haque, Sabir Ismail

In the least developed countries, the lack of spatially explicit and accessible data portals continues to be a significant challenge for policymakers and concerned authorities. The goal of this research is to determine if night-time light (NTL) data can be used to track the development of road infrastructure in space. In this study, Google Maps roads road data (RD) and Day-Night Band (DNB) NTL data from the Visible Infrared Imaging Radiometer Suite (VIIRS) were employed. The Chi-square test of independence was used to assess the relation between VIIRS NTL and RD for two least developed countries, revealing that the variables are interdependent. The Cramer's V test was then used to estimate the strength of the association for both countries as a correlation coefficient. Our findings revealed a correlation value of 0.334 in Bangladesh and 0.299 in Rwanda, suggesting a substantial association between VIIRS NTL and RD. We used the data to perform more exploratory research after finding a statistically significant association.

Methods: Data mining, Statistical analysis

Publication: GIScience 2021

Video: Conference presentation

Where to Build The Next Road?

Collaborators: Sharmin Ahmed, Summit Haque, Sabir Ismail

The goal of this research is to use night-time light (NTL) data to estimate if a certain region requires road infrastructure. For this analysis, we employed Visible Infrared Imaging Radiometer Suite (VIIRS) Day-Night Band (DNB) NTL data from the Visible Infrared Imaging Radiometer Suite (VIIRS), Google day-time Satellite Imagery, and Google Maps Road data (highways). We also used OpenStreetMap shapefiles to extract characteristics like water bodies, health, educational, and financial institutes. Machine learning methods such as XGBoost, AdaBoost, and Random forest, as well as a deep learning model called MLP-CNN, were used to predict road infrastructure. Our XGBoost, AdaBoost, and MLP-CNN models yielded the best F1-score result of 0.88.

Methods: Machine learning, Deep Learning

Publication: Poster accepted at NSysS 2021
